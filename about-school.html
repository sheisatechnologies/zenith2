About School

Amrit Vidya Ashram is an English Medium Co-educational day school affiliated to CBSE Delhi. The school was founded by Amrit Nath Education Trust Kolkata and is managed by the registered body of Amrit Vidya Ashram with its office at Fatehpur Sikar under the noble guidance of Mahant Narhari Nath. The school lays emphasis on spiritual and moral education with a secular base.

The school was established in 2009 and has been developed with generous donations from various donors. This has encouraged the institution's determination to grow and develop this temple of Goddess Saraswati. The school is affiliated (1730580) to the Central Board of Secondary Education, Delhi.

Infrastructure

Lab

The school has well-furnished labs of Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Science, Computer and Maths. Students are encouraged to try experiments independently and enjoy practical learning with the help of teachers. Advanced technology enhances student knowledge practically.

Library

The school library has a rich collection of books, magazines and newspapers to suit the interests of all students. They are encouraged to use the library regularly. New additions are made frequently to keep up with recent publications.

Location

Amrit Vidya Ashram is located in Fatehpur, a unique place in the district of Sikar, Rajasthan. It is situated on the Jaipur–Bikaner National Highway No. 11. Fatehpur is the main railway station between Churu and Sikar and is a historical town attracting foreign tourists. It plays an important role in the cultural and economic life of Rajasthan as well as India.

Our Mission

It is believed that the environment provided by the cultural and religious diversity, which is a policy of the school, furnishes an important factor in fostering unification and brotherhood. The mission of the school is to impart value-based, holistic education that develops students academically, morally and socially.

Rules & Regulations

Rules – School Fee & Leave

1. The School fee is charged quarterly. Re-admission, if granted, will be done on payment of Admission Fee along with late fee.

2. Admission charges are as per school rules.

3. Fee is subject to change even during the session.

4. Once fee is deposited it will not be refunded under any circumstances.

5. Transport charges are to be deposited with school fees. Students cannot opt-out during the term.

6. Parents must obtain permission from the Principal when they want their child to leave school early.

7. Re-opening Days: All students must attend classes on reopening after vacations.

Aims and Objectives

1. To promote education amongst students without any caste or religion discrimination, according to the rules and regulations laid down by the Rajasthan Education Department.

2. To educate students through the present educational system and make them self-reliant and responsible citizens of India.

3. To develop the physical, mental, and intellectual levels of students.

4. To provide relief to poor and needy children.

5. To create awareness amongst students about harmful social systems and develop a sense of secularism and social responsibility.

6. To spread awareness about the benefits of computer education amongst students.

7. To provide fee subsidies to students coming from rural parts of the country and to arrange accommodation and run hostels for them.

8. To create awareness of India’s diverse culture and various Indian art forms amongst students.

9. To organize different sports activities and make students proficient in various sports fields.

10. To organize medical camps from time to time to monitor and improve student health.

11. To celebrate various Indian festivals to teach students to respect all religions and castes.

12. To collect donations for the construction of Ashrams and Schools and raise educational awareness among financially weaker communities.

13. To open institutions in science, engineering, commerce, and arts, and make students proficient in these fields.

14. To obtain recognition from the Central Education Board and the Rajasthan Education Board and function according to the standards set by these boards.

Academics

ACADEMIC SESSION – The academic session begins in the first week of April and concludes in the last week of March. Two vacations are observed:
1) Winter Break: 25 Dec. to 31 Dec.
2) Summer Vacation: 16 May to 30 June.

COURSES OF STUDY – Along with regular CBSE subjects, the school offers Computer Education, Art, Craft, Dance, Music, Work Education, Yoga, Physical Education, and Moral Science.

TEACHING METHODOLOGY – The school follows activity-based learning, digital smart classes, project work, experiential learning, and continuous evaluation to ensure the complete development of the child.

HOME WORK POLICY – Students are given balanced homework to avoid extra pressure and maintain interest in learning.

EXAMINATION SYSTEM – As per CBSE guidelines
Formative Assessment – 1(April – May)
Formative Assessment – 2(July – August)
Summative Assessment – 1(September)
Formative Assessment – 3(November – December)
Formative Assessment – 4(January – February)
Summative Assessment – 2(March)
  • Regular class tests are conducted every month.
  • Notebook submission and subject activities are included in internal assessment.
  • Students must have 75% attendance to appear in final examination.
  • Parents are required to attend Parent-Teacher Meetings after each assessment.
